Meta debuted Muse Spark, its first major large language model developed under chief AI officer Alexandr Wang and the newly formed Meta Superintelligence Labs. The model, internally code-named Avocado, was built over nine months and is designed to significantly narrow the performance gap with frontier models from OpenAI and Anthropic. Notably, Muse Spark achieves its reasoning capabilities using over an order of magnitude less compute than Llama 4 Maverick, Meta's previous mid-size flagship. The release triggered a surge in Meta's stock price, reflecting strong investor enthusiasm for the company's evolving AI strategy.
